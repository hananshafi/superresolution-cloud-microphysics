Overview

Cloud super-resolution needs more than natural-image priors

High-resolution cloud imagery is important for identifying fine-scale structures that matter in cloud microphysics analysis and weather-modification workflows. In practice, the most useful sensors do not align cleanly: geostationary systems offer rapid refresh but coarse resolution, while polar-orbiting systems capture sharper detail with limited revisit frequency.

This paper proposes a cascaded diffusion inversion strategy that separates cross-sensor robustness from high-frequency structural refinement. The result is a better balance of reconstruction fidelity, structural realism, and perceptual quality than transformer and diffusion baselines.

Stage 1

Learns real paired cross-sensor alignment under temporal and geometric mismatch.

Stage 2

Uses HR-only self-supervision with synthetic degradations to recover cloud detail.

Cascade

Combines both strengths to stay sharp without drifting into unreliable texture.

Method

A curriculum that decouples alignment from detail synthesis

Stage 1

Cross-sensor alignment

The first model trains on real paired SEVIRI → VIIRS and MSG → MTG data. It learns a physically consistent mapping despite sensor mismatch, time shift, and imperfect registration.

  • Handles degradation and inter-sensor alignment
  • Improves coarse structural fidelity
  • Builds the prior used by the second stage
Stage 2

Structural refinement

The second model is trained with internally degraded high-resolution data so the input-target relationship is perfectly aligned. This sharpens thin filaments, cloud gaps, and convective textures without depending on noisy real-world pairings.

  • Uses HR-only self-supervision
  • Focuses on fine cloud structure recovery
  • Improves realism while staying close to the target geometry

Abstract

We present a two-stage diffusion-based super-resolution framework for multi-spectral cloud imagery. Stage 1 trains on real paired data to learn robust degradation handling and inter-sensor alignment, while Stage 2 uses self-supervised internal downgrading of high-resolution data to refine structural learning and texture synthesis. Across both SEVIRI → VIIRS and MSG → MTG, the approach outperforms transformer and diffusion baselines in the trade-off between reconstruction accuracy, structural fidelity, and visual quality.

Data and Evaluation

Built for real cross-sensor cloud analysis

Benchmarks

The paper studies two operationally relevant settings: SEVIRI → VIIRS and MSG → MTG.

  • SEVIRI composite uses VIS006, IR016, and IR108 as the closest VIIRS counterparts.
  • MSG → MTG is evaluated through channel-wise paired samples across overlapping bands.
  • Cross-sensor gaps include spectral response, PSF, noise, and time-shift mismatch.

Training setup

Data comes from the UAE Rain Enhancement Program and targets cloud-focused super-resolution rather than natural-image restoration.

  • SEVIRI → VIIRS: roughly 9k training pairs and 1k validation pairs
  • MSG → MTG: roughly 2250 training pairs and 500 validation pairs
  • Both stages were trained on Nvidia A100 40GB GPUs
